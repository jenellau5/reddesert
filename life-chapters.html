Life Chapters

Some stories should not disappear.

Life Chapters is a guided storytelling service where memories, moments, and meaningful parts of life are recorded, transcribed, and turned into a digital keepsake.

Ask About Life Chapters View Pricing

Why it matters

People wait too long to ask the questions that matter.

Childhood stories. Family history. Lessons learned. Funny moments. Hard seasons. What someone wants their kids or grandkids to know. These are the things families often wish they had captured sooner.

Life Chapters gives someone a calm space to talk through their life, their memories, or even the season they are in right now.

How it works

A simple process with a meaningful result.

1

We choose the focus

We talk through what you want captured: life stories, family questions, a certain season, messages, memories, or reflections.

2

I guide the conversation

I ask thoughtful questions, record the conversation, and give the person space to talk naturally without feeling rushed.

3

You receive the keepsake

The conversations are transcribed, lightly edited, and shaped into a digital story keepsake that can be saved, shared, and returned to.

Ways to use it

Not just for one age or stage.

Life Chapters can be used for aging loved ones, parents, grandparents, adults, families, or anyone who wants to preserve something meaningful.

Family History

Capture the stories your family keeps meaning to ask about.

Examples:

  • Childhood memories
  • Family traditions
  • Stories about parents, grandparents, siblings, or marriage
  • Military, career, faith, travel, or life experience stories
  • Answers to questions from children or grandchildren

This Season of Life

For capturing who someone is and what life feels like right now.

Examples:

  • A parent wanting their kids to remember this chapter
  • A major life transition
  • A season of healing, change, or growth
  • Personal reflections someone wants to document
  • Stories that do not need to wait until later

Aging Loved Ones

Preserve stories while they can still be told in their own words.

Examples:

  • Questions before memory starts to fade
  • Stories from earlier decades
  • Life lessons and advice
  • Messages for family
  • Voice and personality captured alongside the story

What may be included

  • Guided conversation
  • Family-submitted questions
  • Audio recording
  • Transcript cleanup
  • Written story format
  • Digital keepsake
  • Selected quotes or meaningful moments
  • Optional photos if provided

What this is not

  • Not therapy
  • Not legal estate planning
  • Not medical care
  • Not a formal biography
  • Not only for end-of-life
  • Not rushed or scripted
