Check-in types

Click a category to see when you would use it.

This is one check-in service that can be used in different situations. The category just helps us understand what kind of concern you have.

Seniors

For aging loved ones who are still independent, but need local eyes.

Use this when:

  • Your parent lives alone and something just feels off
  • They keep saying they are fine, but you are not fully convinced
  • You live out of town and cannot just stop by
  • You want someone to notice small changes before they become big ones
  • You want a real visit, not a rushed box-check

Family & Friends

For someone you care about when you cannot get there yourself.

Use this when:

  • Someone has not answered calls or texts and you are worried
  • A loved one is going through a hard season and you want eyes on them
  • You want to know if they seem okay, not just hear “I’m fine”
  • You do not want to put the responsibility on a neighbor again
  • You need a calm local person to check in and report back

Wellness & Recovery

For the days after surgery, illness, injury, or a rough stretch.

Use this when:

  • Someone is home after surgery and you want reassurance
  • You want to know if they are getting around safely
  • They are recovering, but do not need full-time care
  • You are worried they may downplay how they are really doing
  • You need short-term support during a recovery window

Property & Home

For homes, rentals, offices, or small spaces that need a walkthrough.

Use this when:

  • You are out of town and keep wondering if everything is okay
  • You have a second home sitting empty
  • You want someone to check for obvious issues like leaks, damage, lights, doors, or temperature
  • You do not want to keep asking friends or neighbors to stop by
  • You need a simple eyes-on update with photos when appropriate

Pets

For quick eyes on pets when you are away or need backup.

Use this when:

  • You are on a short trip and want someone to check on your pet
  • You need backup between regular pet care visits
  • You want to know if food, water, or the home setup looks okay
  • You are worried something may be off while you are gone
  • You want a quick update or photo for peace of mind

Custom Check-Ins

For the odd situations that do not fit neatly into a box.

Use this when:

  • You have a one-time concern and need someone local
  • You need something checked on a certain day or time
  • You have a simple task tied to a check-in, like looking at a specific area
  • The situation is not an emergency, but it still matters
  • You need human judgment instead of an app, camera, or automated alert

What a check-in may include

  • In-person visit
  • Basic walkthrough
  • Conversation and connection when appropriate
  • Photos when useful and approved
  • Clear text or email update after
  • Concerns noticed and communicated

What this is not

  • Not emergency response
  • Not nursing care
  • Not medication management
  • Not full-time caregiving
  • Not housekeeping
  • Not a replacement for 911
