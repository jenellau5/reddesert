Check-in types

Click a category to see examples.

This is one check-in service that can be used in different situations. The category just helps us understand what needs to be looked at.

Seniors

For aging loved ones who are still independent but need local eyes.

Examples:

  • Check on an aging parent who lives alone
  • Notice changes in mood, hygiene, food, clutter, or mobility
  • Walk through the home for obvious safety concerns
  • Spend time talking so the visit does not feel clinical
  • Send family a clear update after

Family & Friends

For someone you care about when you cannot get there yourself.

Examples:

  • Check on a loved one who is not responding
  • Drop by when someone is going through a hard season
  • Make sure they have what they need
  • Confirm they are safe and okay
  • Give you a real update, not just “they seem fine”

Wellness & Recovery

Extra eyes during illness, surgery recovery, or temporary support.

Examples:

  • Post-surgery check-in
  • Make sure they are up, eating, and moving safely
  • Look for fall risks or obvious concerns
  • Check that basic needs are being met
  • Update family during a recovery window

Property & Business

For homes, rentals, offices, or small businesses that need a walkthrough.

Examples:

  • Walk through a second home while you are away
  • Check for leaks, damage, open doors, or obvious issues
  • Look at lights, temperature, mail, or general condition
  • Send photos when appropriate
  • Do a simple eyes-on report

Pets

For checking on animals, food, water, safety, and anything that seems off.

Examples:

  • Make sure pets have food and water
  • Check that they appear safe and okay
  • Look for messes, unusual behavior, or concerns
  • Send a quick update or photo
  • Useful for short trips or backup support

Custom Check-Ins

If it does not fit in a box, we can talk through what you need.

Examples:

  • A situation that does not fit the other categories
  • A one-time concern
  • A recurring local check
  • A specific list you want looked at
  • Something that needs human judgment, not an app

What a check-in may include

  • In-person visit
  • Basic walkthrough
  • Conversation and connection when appropriate
  • Photos when useful and approved
  • Clear text or email update after
  • Concerns noticed and communicated

What this is not

  • Not emergency response
  • Not nursing care
  • Not medication management
  • Not full-time caregiving
  • Not housekeeping
  • Not a replacement for 911
